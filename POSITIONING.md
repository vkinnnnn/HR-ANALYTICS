# Positioning — Workforce IQ

Market narrative, value proposition, and why Workforce IQ matters.

---

## The Insight: HR Data is Fragmented

Every organization has employee data scattered across systems:
- **HRIS** (BambooHR, Workday, SuccessFactors) — employee masters, org charts, compensation
- **Performance Reviews** — goals, ratings, feedback
- **Recruiting** (LinkedIn, Greenhouse, Lever) — hiring pipeline, source metrics
- **Finance/Payroll** — headcount budgets, cost per role
- **Learning Management** — training, certifications, skill levels
- **Exit Interviews** — reasons for departure

**The problem:** Nobody connects these dots. HR leaders answer basic questions by manually pulling reports:

> "What's our actual attrition rate by department?"  
> "Which engineers have left in the last year?"  
> "Who's been in the same role for 3+ years?"  
> "Do our best-performing managers retain their teams better?"

**The cost:** Days of Excel, multiple tools open, stale data, no unified narrative.

---

## Workforce IQ: Bloomberg for People Data

Just as Bloomberg terminals revolutionized financial markets by aggregating ticker data into actionable intelligence, **Workforce IQ aggregates employee lifecycle data into strategic people intelligence.**

### Core Thesis
Modern HR teams have access to MORE employee data than ever, but LESS visibility into what it means. The best companies (Google, Netflix, Microsoft) run HR like a business function: metrics-driven, data-first, predictive. The rest remain reactive.

Workforce IQ bridges this gap for mid-market to enterprise organizations.

**Value**: Workspace IQ chatbot answers natural language questions. Analyst asks "Show me tenure distribution in Sales" and gets a chart in 10 seconds.

**Win**: "Moved from reactive reporting (post-departure) to proactive identification (flight risk scoring). Saved 3 critical people in Q1."

**Win**: "Realized Sales had 40% annual turnover. Invested in manager training. Turnover dropped to 18% in 18 months. Saved ~$6M in replacement costs."

## Value Prop by Use Case

### 1. Predictive Attrition ("Who's leaving?")
**Old way:** Exit interview after they're gone.  
**Workforce IQ:** Flight risk ML model identifies likely departures 3-6 months early.

**Impact:**
- Retention conversations before resignation letter
- Skip unnecessary backfills
- Identify systemic problems (broken manager, underpaid role, no growth)

**ROI:** 1 critical person retained = $150K–500K value (loaded salary + ramp + disruption)

### 2. Manager Effectiveness ("Is my leadership investing in people?")
**Old way:** Annual 360 reviews, subjective.  
**Workforce IQ:** Hard data on span of control, report retention, promotion velocity under each manager.

**Impact:**
- Identify "revolving door" managers → coaching or replacement
- Recognize high-retention managers → promotion, leadership programs
- Fair promotion decisions (avoid politics)

**ROI:** One bad manager retained → $500K+ in lost productivity and turnover

### 3. Organizational Bottlenecks ("Where are we stuck?")
**Old way:** Anecdotal: "Engineering has a glass ceiling."  
**Workforce IQ:** Data shows 47% of engineers promoted to manager, but only 3% to director (vs 12% in other functions).

**Impact:**
- Redesign career ladders
- Adjust compensation for stuck levels
- Identify skill gaps for hiring

**ROI:** Improve engineer retention 5% → $1M+ per 100 engineers

### 4. Workforce Planning ("Do we have the right shape?")
**Old way:** Headcount forecasts based on gut.  
**Workforce IQ:** Historical tenure, promotion velocity, attrition by level feeds predictive hiring models.

**Impact:**
- Avoid over-hiring (most companies hire 15–25% excess annually)
- Right-size IC vs management tiers
- Plan succession for retirement wave

**ROI:** 10% reduction in hiring excess → $2M+ savings per 500 employees

### 5. Compliance & Diversity ("Are we equitable?")
**Old way:** Manual audits, self-reported data.  
**Workforce IQ:** Standardized job families, promotion rates by grade/function/location/protected class.

**Impact:**
- Spot pay equity issues early
- Track progress on diversity commitments
- Support legal defense with data

**ROI:** Avoid $500K+ discrimination litigation + reputational risk

## Appendix: Benchmarks & Industry Context

### HR Analytics Market
- **Market size**: $3.5B globally (2023), growing 12% CAGR
- **Key insight**: 60% of companies still use spreadsheets as primary analytics tool
- **Trend**: Shift from reactive (post-departure) to predictive (pre-departure)

### Attrition Costs
- **Tech industry**: 150% of annual salary (hiring, training, lost productivity)
- **Average company**: 50–200% depending on role level
- **Saving 1 critical person**: $150K–$500K value

### Adoption Barriers
- **Workday dominance**: 40% of large enterprises, but analytics modules are expensive add-ons
- **Fragmentation**: Average company uses 8–12 HR systems; no unified source of truth
- **Talent scarcity**: Not enough data engineers to build custom analytics for 80% of mid-market

### Why Now
1. **AI democratization**: OpenAI APIs make natural language search accessible
2. **Data availability**: More companies now export CSVs, APIs from HRIS
3. **Talent market shift**: Post-COVID, retention is CEO priority #1
4. **Regulatory pressure**: DEI reporting, pay equity audits mandate data visibility
