## 1. The Problem (2 min)

### Current State: HR Leaders Fly Blind
- **Attrition surprise:** 15% of your team leaves unexpectedly. You didn't see it coming.
- **Flight risk invisible:** Your best people are frustrated. No early warning system.

## 3. Key Features (3 min)

### Dashboard — Workforce at a Glance
**KPIs:**
- Active headcount + new hires this quarter
- Turnover rate (company + by department)
- Avg tenure (overall + by grade)
- Flight risk employees flagged

**Charts:**
- Headcount trend line (12-month history)
- Turnover heatmap (red zones highlighted)
- Tenure distribution (career stage visibility)
- Flight risk watchlist (top 10 with context)

---

### Turnover & Attrition
- Attrition rate by every dimension (dept, grade, location, function)
- Tenure-at-departure histogram (early flight vs. long-tenured departures)
- Danger zones flagged (>20% turnover = at-risk department)
- Voluntary vs. involuntary detection
- Monthly trends (spot rising attrition early)

---

### Flight Risk — ML-Powered Prediction
**Features used:**
- Tenure (newer = higher risk)
- Time in current role (stagnation = risk)
- Manager changes (instability = risk)
- Grade progression (blocked advancement = risk)
- Department turnover (contagion effect = risk)

**Output:**
- Risk scores (0–100%) for all active employees
- Feature importance (what drives *this person's* risk?)
- Top 10 watchlist with dept/role context
- Risk by department heatmap (find clusters)

---

### Career Progression
- Promotion velocity (avg days between title changes)
- Career path visualization (engineer → manager → director → VP)
- Stuck talent list (3+ years same role)
- Lateral vs. upward move ratio (growth opportunity)
- Grade progression timeline (where do people advance?)

---

### Manager Analytics
- Span of control distribution (who has too many reports?)
- Manager retention scorecard (% of reports who stay 1yr, 2yr, 3yr)
- Revolving door detection (managers with high churn)
- Manager-to-employee ratio by department
- Insights: "Manager A retains 95% of reports. Manager B loses 60%. What's different?"

---

### Org Structure
- Department size trends (growing/shrinking)
- Hierarchy depth analysis (org layers)
- Restructuring event detection (when did moves happen?)
- Interactive org tree (who reports to whom?)
- Benchmarking (your span vs. industry norm?)
